## Problem Statement

The strategy can build substantial **open profit** during strong momentum moves, particularly when pyramiding is active.  
However, during volatility spikes or sharp pullbacks, trailing stops may allow a **large percentage of that profit to be given back** before an exit occurs.

While this behavior is sometimes acceptable in trend-following systems, on short timeframes (e.g. 1‑minute futures scalping) it introduces:

- Excessive profit volatility,
- Psychological frustration (large unrealized → small realized),
- Session-level expectancy degradation even when individual trades are “winners”.

There is currently **no explicit cap on profit give-back** once a trade has achieved a meaningful unrealized gain.

---

## Desired Outcome

- Protect a configurable portion of **maximum open profit** once achieved.
- Ensure that large winners do not degrade into marginal outcomes.
- Apply protection **without forcing early exits** or interfering with normal trailing behavior.
- Operate as a **governor**, not a hard take-profit.
