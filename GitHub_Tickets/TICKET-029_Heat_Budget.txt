## Problem Statement

Current risk controls operate at the **per-entry level** (fixed SL per contract) but do not cap **aggregate exposure** across pyramided positions.

This allows scenarios where:
- Many contracts are open simultaneously,
- Volatility expands,
- Multiple stops are triggered together,
causing **large, sudden give-backs** that exceed intended trade risk.

This violates core risk principles:  
> Total risk = position size × stop distance × volatility.

---

## Desired Outcome

- Enforce a hard cap on **total open risk (“heat”)** per trade.
- Prevent new scale-ins when aggregate exposure exceeds budget.
- Preserve pyramiding in smooth trends while blocking risk spikes.
- Make exposure behavior volatility-aware.

---

## Requirements

### 1. Heat Budget Definition
Define:
- `MaxHeatTicks` (configurable, default e.g. 30 MNQ ticks)
- Heat per contract = distance from entry to its stop (in ticks)
- Total Heat = sum of heat across all open contracts
